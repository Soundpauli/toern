Draw mode (the default view)

Draw mode is what greets you on power-up. Every voice is visible at full intensity; the red playhead line runs across all rows at once; the cursor at (current_x, current_y) is the focus point.

Single mode (focus on one voice)

Same logic as draw, but only the selected voice is drawn at full intensity; everything else fades to its base tint. Good for working on a single instrument without visual noise from the others.

Single mode: focus voice is bright white, neighbouring voices show as dim base colours.

Enter single mode with hold L (touch 1) from draw (hold again to return). All gestures from draw apply, but: copy/paste only grabs the focused channel; the clear-page chord (encoder 1 + encoder 4 both long) only clears the focused channel; randomize from the page row affects only the focused channel.

Set triggers & remove triggers

Every lit step on the grid is a trigger — “play this voice here.” Placing steps is paint; clearing them is unpaint. How your hands do that depends on SETTINGS → DRAW.

L+R vs R — pick a handedness

Both modes use the same grid. The difference is which encoder writes and which one erases (or does something else).

Flip the mode anytime under SETTINGS → DRAW. Your pattern stays put — only the gestures change.

Set triggers (paint) — L+R

Left hand = which voice. Right hand = which step, and the pen that writes.

  • Turn encoder 1 — move to another voice row.
  • Turn encoder 4 — move along time (steps).
  • Tap encoder 4 — place a trigger at the cursor.
  • Hold encoder 4 and turn — paint a continuous trail of triggers as you move.

L+R paint: enc 4 writes along the timeline while enc 1 picks the lane.

Remove triggers (unpaint) — L+R

Same mode, opposite hand: encoder 1 is the eraser.

  • Tap encoder 1 — clear the trigger under the cursor.
  • Hold encoder 1 and turn — wipe a trail of triggers.

You never “toggle” with one knob in L+R — write and erase stay on separate encoders so you can’t accidentally flip the wrong way mid-gesture.

R mode — set & remove on one encoder

With DRAW = R, encoder 4 does both jobs: tap an empty cell to set, tap a lit cell to remove. Hold and turn to paint or erase a trail — the latch follows whatever was under the cursor when you pressed.

  • Tap encoder 4 — toggle that cell (empty ↔ trigger).
  • Hold encoder 4 and turn — continuous paint if you started on empty, continuous erase if you started on a trigger.
  • Turn encoder 1 — still chooses the voice row.

R mode: one hand on enc 4 for set/remove; enc 1 is free for breaks.

R mode bonus — temporary full mute

Because erase moved off encoder 1, that encoder’s press becomes a performance mute: everything goes silent so you can drop a break. How you leave the mute decides what comes back:

  • Encoder 1 again — restore the mutes you had before
  • Encoder 2 — only channels 1 and 2 unmuted
  • Encoder 3 — apply the custom set from SETTINGS → MUTE
  • Encoder 4 — unmute everything

SETTINGS → MUTE is that custom “come back to these lanes” mask — not a gate on which rows you can paint. Build a “kick + snare only” return if you want breaks that land on just those voices.

Need to wipe a whole page? On the page row, unpaint that page’s strip LED with encoder 1 (L+R) — see Pages+More → Delete a page. Or use the enc 1 + enc 4 long chord / single-mode clear on the current edit page.
