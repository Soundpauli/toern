Sequencing

Draw, single, L+R paint, R-mode, and the page row — with hint colours and every encoder action spelled out.

Draw mode (the default view)

Draw mode is what greets you on power-up. Every voice is visible at full intensity; the red playhead line runs across all rows at once; the cursor at (current_x, current_y) is the focus point.

Single mode (focus on one voice)

Same logic as draw, but only the selected voice is drawn at full intensity; everything else fades to its base tint. Good for working on a single instrument without visual noise from the others.

Single mode: focus voice is bright white, neighbouring voices show as dim base colours.

Enter single mode with hold L (touch 1) from draw (hold again to return). All gestures from draw apply, but: copy/paste only grabs the focused channel; the clear-page chord (encoder 1 + encoder 4 both long) only clears the focused channel; randomize from the page row affects only the focused channel.

Paint mode (two-hand draw)

With PLAY → DRAW set to L+R (the default), writing notes is a two-hand job. Your left hand on encoder 1 chooses the row (which voice). Your right hand on encoder 4 chooses the column (which step) and does the writing.

  • Tap encoder 4 — place a note at the cursor.
  • Hold encoder 4 and turn — paint a trail of notes as you move through time (and change row with encoder 1 while you hold).
  • Tap encoder 1 — erase the note under the cursor. Hold and turn to wipe a trail.

Think of encoder 4 as the pen and encoder 1 as which stave you are drawing on. A lot of people like this for live playing because each hand owns one axis.

R-mode (one-hand draw)

Flip PLAY → DRAW to R when you want everything on one hand. Encoder 4 alone places, erases, and paints; encoder 1 is no longer the eraser.

  • Tap encoder 4 — toggle the cell under the cursor (empty → note, note → empty).
  • Hold encoder 4 — if the cell is empty, latch continuous paint; if it already has a note, latch continuous erase. Keep holding and turn to sweep.
  • Press encoder 1 (short or long, on a voice row) — temporary full mute: everything goes silent so you can drop a break. How you leave that mute decides what comes back:
    • Encoder 1 again → restore the mutes you had before
    • Encoder 2 → only channels 1 and 2 unmuted
    • Encoder 3 → apply the custom set from PLAY → MUTE
    • Encoder 4 → unmute everything

PLAY → MUTE is that custom unmute set for the exit above — not a gate on which rows you can paint. Use it to define a “kick + snare only” return from a full mute.

Need a page-wide clear? Use the page-row tools in single mode (or the enc 1 + enc 4 long chord in draw) — those are the intentional wipe paths.

The page row

Turn encoder 1 until the cursor sits on the top strip of the matrix — that is the page row (row 16). It shows your 16 pattern pages as a row of dots, and it unlocks the tools that act on a whole page or a whole voice. On any voice row those same presses do something else (or nothing).

You are on the page row when the hint strip on row 1 changes colour and the matching encoder rings light up. That colour change is the cue that power tools are armed.

What lives here:

  • Copy & paste a page (draw = all voices; single = focused voice only)
  • Clear page for the focused voice — single mode only
  • Random fill for the focused voice — single mode only
  • Note-shift — slide the focused voice in time / across rows — single mode only

Copy armed

After the first encoder 4 tap, the page strip goes dark and the hints jump to large blue (enc 1) and large yellow (enc 4). That means the buffer is full — navigate to the destination page, then tap encoder 4 again to paste. Another tap while armed without pasting toggles the armed state off.

Copy armed: page strip cleared, blue + yellow large cues.

Single mode on the page row

Hints: red (enc 1), white (enc 2), yellow (enc 4). Same copy/paste idea, but it only grabs the focused voice — so you can copy a hi-hat lane and paste it onto another voice after you change focus.

Note-shift

From single mode + page row, short-press the white encoder 2. The firmware snapshots every other voice, then lets you slide only the focused voice. Hints switch to bright green (enc 1) and bright blue (enc 4). A grey marquee crawls on row 1 while you work.

1 Encoder 1 · green
TurnShift pitch / rowMoves the focused voice’s notes up and down across the whole pattern (wraps at the edges).
2 Encoder 2
TurnShift pitch on this page onlySame up/down idea, but only inside the current edit page.
3 Encoder 3
ShortPlay / pauseTransport still works while shifting.
4 Encoder 4 · blue
TurnShift in timeSlides the focused voice left/right across the whole pattern (wraps).
ShortExitCommits the shift and returns to single mode.

From a voice row, encoder 2 short does not enter note-shift — the white cue on the page row is the reminder that this tool is page-row only.

Mutes

From a voice row (not the page row), long-press encoder 2 opens a transient mute overlay: while you hold it you can flip lanes; release to return. Short-press encoder 2 on a voice row toggles that lane’s mute and the flag sticks.

Mute overlay: muted lanes dim, active lanes bright.

On the page row, encoder 2 short is note-shift (single mode) — not mute. Step onto a voice row first if you want the overlay.

Random & auto-fill

Where in-grid randomize works: single mode + cursor on the page row, with the red encoder 1 cue lit. Long-press encoder 1. On a voice row the same long press is erase-trail / other context — not randomize.

That long press writes a fresh pattern for the focused voice: scale-aware notes, density similar to what was already on the page. Repeat for a new roll; clear with encoder 1 short and try again.

For multi-page generative fill, use ETC → AUTO in the menu — that one is a menu entry, not a grid gesture.

Transpose vs note shift

Note-shift moves real step data in time and across rows — it edits the pattern. Enter it from single mode + page row with the white encoder 2 (see above). Encoder 4 slides in time; encoder 1 slides pitch across the whole pattern; encoder 2 slides pitch on the current page only.

There is no global transpose page. To change pitch without rewriting steps, use detune and octave in Sound design. Use note-shift when you want the melody to start on a different beat or sit on a different row.
